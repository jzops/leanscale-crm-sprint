## Email Best Practices

**Subject Lines:**
- Keep under 40 characters
- Lowercase, conversational
- Questions work well
- Avoid "CRM Implementation" — too formal

**Body:**
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- One idea per email
- Always include clear CTA
- Use "Joe" not "The LeanScale Team"

**Timing:**
- Send 8-9 AM recipient timezone
- Weekdays only
- Respect unsubscribes immediately

### Branch: No Engagement (0 Opens After 2 Emails)

**Trigger:** Contact has received 2 or more emails AND has opened 0 of them.

**Action:** Pause the current sequence. Switch to the alternate subject line variations below and adjust send times. Try sending at 7:00 AM, 12:00 PM, and 5:30 PM recipient timezone on subsequent attempts. If still no opens after 3 more attempts, remove from automation.

**Alternate Subject Line Variations:**

Use these shorter, curiosity-driven subject lines in place of the standard ones:

1. **"Quick question"** — Works across all sequences. Simple, creates curiosity, looks like a real 1:1 email. Pair with a very short body (2-3 sentences max).

2. **"Saw this and thought of you"** — Implies relevance without revealing content. Body should reference something specific to their industry or role.

3. **"Worth 2 minutes?"** — Low commitment ask. Body should lead with a single stat or provocative claim, then link to the CTA.

**Re-engagement email template:**

**Subject:** (use one of the three variations above)

**Body:**

Hey {{first_name}},

**Send time rules:**
- Attempt 1: 7:00 AM recipient timezone (catch early inbox checkers)
- Attempt 2: 12:00 PM recipient timezone (lunch break scan)
- Attempt 3: 5:30 PM recipient timezone (end-of-day sweep)

### Branch: Replied But Didn't Book

**Trigger:** Contact replied to any email in the sequence AND has NOT booked a call within 48 hours of replying.

**Action:** Immediately remove from all automated sequences. Move to manual 1:1 follow-up queue.

**Handoff Process:**

1. **Automation tag:** Apply tag `replied-no-book` in the CRM. This removes them from all active nurture sequences.

2. **Slack notification:** Send an alert to the `#sales-replies` Slack channel with:
   - Contact name, company, title
   - Which email they replied to
   - Full text of their reply
   - Link to their CRM record

3. **Owner assignment:** Assign the contact to Joe (or the designated sales rep) as the primary owner.

4. **Response SLA:** Manual reply must go out within 4 business hours. If the reply came outside business hours, respond by 9:00 AM next business day.

5. **Follow-up cadence (manual):**
   - Day 0: Personalized reply to their message. Acknowledge what they said. Ask one clarifying question. Include Calendly link naturally (not as a hard push).
   - Day 2: If no response, short follow-up. Reference their original reply. Offer a specific time ("Are you free Thursday at 2pm?").
   - Day 5: Final manual follow-up. Offer to send over relevant info async if a call doesn't work.
   - Day 10: If still no booking, add back to a low-frequency monthly newsletter. Do NOT re-enter them into a nurture sequence.

6. **CRM logging:** Every manual touchpoint must be logged. Use activity type "Manual Follow-Up" with notes on context.
