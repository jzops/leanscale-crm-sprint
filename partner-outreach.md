# CRM Sprint — Partner Outreach

## Partner Categories

### 1. HubSpot Solutions Partners
These are agencies/consultants already in the HubSpot ecosystem. Many don't do quick-turn implementation work.

### 2. Fractional CROs / VP Sales
Consultants who advise on sales strategy but don't do technical implementation.

### 3. VC Operating Partners
Portfolio support teams who need a go-to partner for CRM work.

### 4. Salesforce Partners Who Don't Do SMB
Enterprise-focused SI partners who get inbound from smaller companies.

### Fractional CROs / VP Sales (5-7)

Rather than targeting specific individuals (who change roles frequently), build a prospect list using these sourcing methods:

**Where to find them:**
- **Pavilion (formerly Revenue Collective):** Search the member directory for "Fractional CRO," "Fractional VP Sales," or "Interim Sales Leader." Filter for members who serve B2B SaaS companies with 20-200 employees.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use the search query: `"fractional CRO" OR "fractional VP Sales" OR "interim head of sales"` filtered to your target geography. Look for profiles that mention advising Series A-C startups.
- **TopFrac / GrowthAssistant / Bolster:** Fractional executive marketplaces where CROs list their profiles.

**Ideal profile:**
- 10-15+ years in B2B sales leadership
- Currently advising 2-5 startups simultaneously
- Active on LinkedIn (posts about sales process, pipeline management, hiring)
- Has a track record at venture-backed companies
- Does NOT offer CRM implementation as part of their services (this is the gap we fill)

**Target: Identify 5-7 fractional CROs and add to outreach list in Week 1.**

## Referral Fee Structure

**Option A: Fixed Fee**
- $2,500 per closed deal
- Paid within 30 days of project kickoff

**Option B: Revenue Share**
- 10% of project value
- Paid within 30 days of final payment received

**Option C: Reciprocal**
- No cash exchange
- We refer work to each other when appropriate
- Track referrals quarterly

**Recommendation:** Start with Option C for strategic partners (VC operating partners, fractional CROs). Use Option A or B for transactional referrals.

## Co-Marketing Playbook

Joint marketing activities give partners a reason to stay engaged beyond referral fees. Each activity should deliver value to the partner's audience while positioning CRM Sprint as the go-to implementation partner.

### Joint Webinar Templates

**Webinar 1: "Setting Up GTM Infrastructure Post-Raise"**
- **Best partner type:** VC Operating Partners
- **Format:** 45 min live webinar + Q&A
- **Speakers:** LeanScale lead + VC operating partner
- **Content outline:**
  1. What investors expect from GTM infrastructure in the first 90 days
  2. CRM, pipeline tracking, and reporting essentials for Series A/B
  3. Common mistakes founders make with their first CRM setup
  4. Live demo: what a "Day 30" CRM should look like
- **CTA:** Free GTM readiness audit for portfolio companies

**Webinar 2: "Aligning Sales Process and CRM Before You Hire Rep #3"**
- **Best partner type:** Fractional CROs / VP Sales
- **Format:** 30 min fireside chat
- **Speakers:** LeanScale lead + fractional CRO
- **Content outline:**
  1. Why CRM breaks when you go from 2 to 5 reps
  2. How to design a sales process that scales
  3. What to automate vs. what to keep manual
  4. The handoff: strategy (fractional CRO) + implementation (LeanScale)
- **CTA:** Free CRM audit for the fractional CRO's current clients

**Webinar 3: "From Inbound Leads to Closed Revenue: Connecting Marketing and Sales in HubSpot"**
- **Best partner type:** HubSpot Solutions Partners (marketing-focused)
- **Format:** 45 min co-presented webinar
- **Speakers:** LeanScale lead + HubSpot partner lead
- **Content outline:**
  1. Where leads get lost between marketing and sales
  2. HubSpot lifecycle stages and lead routing done right
  3. Reporting that connects marketing spend to closed revenue
  4. Division of labor: marketing ops (partner) + sales ops (LeanScale)
- **CTA:** Joint assessment — marketing + sales ops health check

### Co-Branded Content Pieces

**Piece 1: "The Post-Funding CRM Playbook" (PDF)**
- **Best partner type:** VC Operating Partners
- **Format:** 15-20 page PDF guide, co-branded with VC firm logo
- **Distribution:** VC shares with portfolio companies; LeanScale uses in outbound
- **Sections:** Choosing a CRM, implementation timeline, must-have integrations, first 90 days of reporting, when to hire RevOps vs. outsource

**Piece 2: "The Sales Leader's CRM Checklist" (PDF)**
- **Best partner type:** Fractional CROs / VP Sales
- **Format:** 2-page checklist, co-branded
- **Distribution:** Fractional CRO shares with clients; both parties post on LinkedIn
- **Sections:** 20-point checklist covering pipeline stages, automation, reporting, and rep adoption

**Piece 3: "HubSpot vs. Salesforce for Series A-C Companies" (Blog Post)**
- **Best partner type:** HubSpot Solutions Partners
- **Format:** Long-form blog post (1,500-2,000 words), published on both sites
- **Distribution:** Cross-linked blog posts, shared on LinkedIn, included in both email newsletters

### Joint Case Studies

- Co-write a case study for every deal that comes through a partner referral
- Structure: Client challenge > Partner's role (strategy/intro) > LeanScale's role (implementation) > Results
- Both parties get a branded version to use in their sales process
- **Goal:** 1 joint case study per quarter per active partner

### Cross-Promotion on LinkedIn

**Tagging strategy:**
- When posting about CRM implementation tips, tag relevant partners: "This is something I talk about a lot with @PartnerName — they handle the strategy side, we handle the build."
- When a partner posts about sales process or GTM, comment with a relevant CRM implementation angle and tag LeanScale.
- Share partner content with added commentary at least 1x per week.

**Shared post cadence:**
- 1x/month: Co-authored LinkedIn post (alternating who publishes — tag each other)
- 1x/quarter: Celebrate a joint win (anonymized case study, metric, or milestone)
- Ongoing: Comment on and reshare each other's content

- [ ] Finalize the target partner list (use the Target Partner List section above as a starting point)

- [ ] Personalize each outreach template with specific details (e.g., reference a recent blog post or webinar they did)
- [ ] Set up partner tracking table in CRM (or spreadsheet if CRM isn't ready)
- [ ] Prepare the partner one-pager PDF to share during onboarding
- [ ] Identify 5-7 fractional CROs using LinkedIn Sales Navigator and Pavilion directory
- **Deliverable:** 25-30 personalized outreach messages ready to send

### Weeks 3-4: First Batch of Outreach (10 Partners)

- [ ] Send outreach to first 10 partners (mix of all 4 categories)
  - 4 HubSpot Solutions Partners
  - 2 Fractional CROs
  - 2 VC Operating Partners
  - 2 Enterprise Salesforce Partners
- [ ] Log all outreach in partner tracking table with dates
- [ ] Begin Day 5 follow-up sequence for non-responders
- [ ] Monitor LinkedIn for engagement (profile views, connection requests)

### Weeks 5-6: Follow Up & Schedule Intro Calls

- [ ] Send Day 12 follow-up to remaining non-responders
- [ ] Schedule intro calls with all partners who responded positively
- [ ] Conduct intro calls (15 min each): explain CRM Sprint, referral structure, co-marketing opportunity
- [ ] Send partner one-pager to all who take a call
- [ ] Identify warmest partners and prioritize for onboarding
- [ ] Connect with partners on LinkedIn; begin engaging with their content
- **Deliverable:** 3-5 intro calls completed, 2-3 partners moving to onboarding

### Weeks 7-8: Onboard First Partners & Send First Referral

- [ ] Complete onboarding for first 2-3 partners (fee structure agreed, tracking set up)
- [ ] Send each onboarded partner one referral to demonstrate value (even if it's a warm intro, not a full deal)
- [ ] Share partner one-pager so they can forward to their clients
- [ ] Kick off first co-marketing conversation (propose a joint webinar or co-branded PDF)
- [ ] Begin second batch of outreach prep (next 10 partners)

### Weeks 9-12: Second Batch & First Co-Marketing Activity

- [ ] Send outreach to second batch of 10 partners
- [ ] Follow the same follow-up sequence (Day 5, Day 12)
- [ ] Publish or schedule first co-marketing piece (webinar, PDF, or co-authored LinkedIn post)
- [ ] Conduct first quarterly check-in with onboarded partners (even if it's only been 4-6 weeks)
- [ ] Review partner pipeline: which partners are sending referrals? Which need re-engagement?
- [ ] Update partner tracking table with referral counts and revenue attribution
- [ ] Begin planning Q2 co-marketing calendar
- **Deliverable:** 20 total partners contacted, first co-marketing activity live

## Partner Success Metrics

Track these KPIs monthly and review them in quarterly partner program reviews.

### Primary KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target (Q1) | Target (Steady State) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Active Partners** | Partners who have sent at least 1 referral or participated in co-marketing in the last 90 days | 10 | 20-25 |
| **Referrals Received per Quarter** | Total inbound referrals from all partners | 10-15 | 30-50 |
| **Referral-to-Close Rate** | % of partner referrals that convert to signed deals | 30-40% | 40-50% |
| **Revenue from Partner-Sourced Deals** | Total revenue attributed to partner referrals (quarterly) | $50K-$75K | $200K+ |
| **Partner Satisfaction Score** | Qualitative score (1-5) from quarterly check-in conversations | 4.0+ | 4.5+ |

### Secondary KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Outreach-to-Partner Rate** | % of partners contacted who become active partners | 30-40% |
| **Time to First Referral** | Days from partner onboarding to first referral sent (either direction) | < 30 days |
| **Co-Marketing Activities per Quarter** | Webinars, co-branded content, joint posts published | 2-3 |
| **Referral Revenue per Partner** | Average revenue generated per active partner per quarter | $10K-$15K |
| **Bi-Directional Referral Rate** | % of partnerships where referrals flow both directions | 50%+ |

### Quarterly Partner Check-In Agenda

Use this template for the quarterly review conversation with each active partner:

1. **Referral review:** How many referrals sent/received? Any in pipeline?
2. **Quality check:** Are the referrals a good fit? Any issues with handoff or client experience?
3. **Co-marketing review:** What have we done together? What should we do next quarter?
4. **Satisfaction:** On a scale of 1-5, how would you rate the partnership? What would make it a 5?
5. **Growth opportunities:** Any new contacts, events, or channels we should explore together?

### Reporting Cadence

- **Weekly:** Update partner tracking table (new referrals, status changes)
- **Monthly:** Review primary KPIs; flag underperforming partnerships for re-engagement
- **Quarterly:** Full partner program review; conduct partner check-ins; plan next quarter's co-marketing calendar
